\section{Pipeline overview}
\label{sec:trad-overview}

The traditional pipeline mirrors the industrial monitoring stack (SAGE/ADEM-class systems). Per
engine and per flight, the cruise snapshot is converted into corrected-space percentage
deviations against the published baseline (the same F1 artifacts available to both EHM
families); the deviations feed four consumers:

\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Smoothing} (Holt double exponential, level + trend): the airline-facing trend
    plots, and the source of \emph{innovations} --- one-step forecast errors, which are the
    correct series for event detection because the chronic trend has been absorbed by the
    level and trend states.
  \item \textbf{Detection}: a CUSUM on the innovations (catches steps: FOD, abrupt faults)
    combined with a dual-EWMA \emph{gap} detector --- fast EWMA minus slow EWMA --- whose gap
    opens by roughly the fault magnitude during a ramp (catches acute episodes). Alarms
    require persistence ($k$-of-$n$) and, for EGT, are one-sided: washes pull the deviation
    down, and improvement is not a fault.
  \item \textbf{Health estimation}: snapshot WLS-GPA (\cref{eq:wls}) and a random-walk
    Kalman-GPA tracker (\cref{eq:kalman}) with the operating-point-interpolated ICM. The
    tracker is \emph{wash-aware}: at logged wash events (maintenance records are available to
    a real EHM system too) the recoverable compressor states are pulled toward zero and their
    covariance reopened.
  \item \textbf{Isolation and prognosis}: on detection, the deviation step across the alarm is
    matched against the single-parameter ICM signatures (nearest-cosine expert rule); the
    remaining useful life comes from a robust Theil--Sen extrapolation of the tracked takeoff
    EGT margin to zero, with a NASA-style horizon cap.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Two detector-design lessons}
\label{sec:trad-lessons}

Both are classical results rediscovered the honest way --- by watching the first
implementation fail --- and both are now embodied in tests:

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{CUSUM on the raw deviation is wrong for aging systems.} The first run applied
    CUSUM to the EGT deviation itself: the chronic trend integrates without bound, so
    \emph{every} engine alarmed mid-life (detection recall 0, every healthy engine a false
    alarm). Step detectors belong on innovations, where the expected value under chronic aging
    is zero.
  \item \textbf{A Holt trend-shift detector cannot see acute ramps.} At realistic smoothing
    gains the noise of the estimated trend exceeds the slope added by a 50--500-cycle ramp
    entirely. The dual-EWMA gap detector solves this: over the ramp the fast average follows
    and the slow one lags, opening a gap of the order of the full fault magnitude ---
    signal-to-noise an order of magnitude better than the trend estimate.
\end{itemize}

\begin{description}
  \item[Detection is fast but partial.] Detected episodes alarm within $\sim$100 cycles
    (against ramps of 50--500), but recall is 0.5: the missed episodes are dominated by the
    weak-signature faults ($\Gamma_{\mathrm{HPT}}$ moves EGT by only $+0.11$\,\% per percent
    --- half a noise standard deviation) and by drift-contaminated engines.
  \item[Isolation collapses exactly where the ICM said it would.] Overall isolation is
    40\,\%; on the confusable subset it is 0\,\%. \Cref{fig:kf-smearing} shows the mechanism
    live: the Kalman tracker follows the \emph{measurements} faithfully but splits the acute
    step between the faulted parameter and its 1.3$^{\circ}$ partner --- the smearing index
    says 85\,\% of the estimated magnitude lands on healthy components. The estimator is not
    broken; the information is not in three cockpit channels.
  \item[Margin extrapolation is optimistic early.] \Cref{fig:rul-funnel}: at half-life the
    median RUL error is $+7\,600$ cycles, shrinking to $+1\,900$ at 90\,\% of life. Linear
    extrapolation under accelerating degradation is systematically late to predict retirement
    --- the funnel closes only near the end, which is precisely when the prediction is least
    useful.
\end{description}

\section{Milestone H3}
\label{sec:h3}

The gate requires the traditional pipeline to run end-to-end on the test fleet and produce the
common metric set --- met above ($\sim$2 minutes per fleet, one worker per engine). Its
defaults and their rationale are itemized in the decision register
(\cref{ch:decisions}); every one of them is exposed to the F5 tuning budget. H3 is declared
closed with this documented status: \textbf{these are floor numbers, not the traditional
family's final word} --- treating them otherwise would manufacture exactly the straw-man
comparison this project exists to avoid.
