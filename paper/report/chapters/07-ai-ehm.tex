\chapter{The AI EHM suite}
\label{ch:ai}

\begin{quote}\itshape
This chapter in brief: the AI suite consumes exactly the same deviations as the traditional
pipeline and is scored by the same protocol. In its untuned v0 form it already wins the
prognosis contest decisively --- 4--6$\times$ lower RUL error with calibrated uncertainty
intervals --- while its detection and diagnosis attempts surface two instructive failures,
and the first physics-informed hybrid experiment produces a pre-registration-grade
\emph{negative} result. All of it is reported.
\end{quote}

\section{Fairness by construction}
\label{sec:ai-fairness}

Every AI model in this chapter sees, per flight, the same four numbers the traditional
pipeline sees: the three cockpit deviations (N2, $W_f$, EGT) against the same published
baseline, plus the takeoff EGT deviation. No extra sensors, no truth channels, no future
leakage (all windows end at the prediction time). Missing snapshots are forward-filled, as a
monitoring unit holding its last valid report would. Training uses the train engines only;
thresholds and conformal calibration use the validation engines; every number below comes
from the 20 test engines. Torch models train on the Apple-silicon GPU (MPS backend, norm N1);
tree models are scikit-learn's histogram gradient boosting.\footnote{XGBoost was evicted for
an unglamorous reason recorded in the decision register: its bundled OpenMP runtime
segfaults alongside torch-MPS on macOS. One runtime, no crash, same model family.}

\section{Prognosis: the headline result}
\label{sec:ai-rul}

A two-layer GRU consumes a downsampled 1\,280-cycle deviation history and regresses the
remaining useful life (capped at 12\,000 cycles, the standard prognostics practice).
\Cref{fig:rul-compare} and \cref{tab:ai-vs-trad} give the v0 face-off against the
margin-extrapolation prognosis of \cref{ch:trad}:

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{4--6$\times$ lower RMSE} at every evaluation point (1\,547 vs.\ 9\,084 cycles
    at half-life; 702 vs.\ 2\,375 at 90\,\%).
  \item \textbf{The optimism bias is gone.} The traditional median error of $+7\,612$ cycles
    at half-life ---late retirement predictions, the dangerous direction--- collapses to
    $-393$; the GRU learns the acceleration of late-life degradation that a linear
    extrapolation structurally cannot represent.
  \item \textbf{Calibrated uncertainty.} Split-conformal intervals at 90\,\% nominal coverage
    achieve 0.85/0.95/1.00 empirical coverage on test with $\pm2\,022$-cycle half-width
    (contribution C4's machinery, live). The traditional pipeline's Theil--Sen slope
    dispersion offers no comparable guarantee.
\end{itemize}

\section{Detection and diagnosis: two instructive failures}
\label{sec:ai-failures}

\textbf{The naive window-autoencoder scores recall 0.} Trained on acute-free windows, it
reconstructs \emph{everything} well --- including post-fault windows. Root cause: a dense
autoencoder over raw deviation windows learns a near-identity map, and a sustained level
shift is statistically indistinguishable from the chronic drift it was trained to compress.
Anomaly detection by reconstruction needs either residual whitening or change-relative
features; the Isolation Forest on step features (fast-minus-slow averages) is the right
shape of fix but at its conservative v0 threshold also detects nothing sustained. Detection
remains the traditional pipeline's win at v0 (recall 0.5 vs.\ 0.0) --- an honest scoreboard
entry with a clear engineering path.

\textbf{Diagnosis is sample-starved at the engine level for both families.} The gradient
boosting classifier reaches 20\,\% isolation accuracy vs.\ the expert rule's 40\,\% --- but
these are 2 vs.\ 4 correct out of \emph{ten} test engines, drawn from only 35 acute training
engines spread over six fault classes. No classifier learns six signatures from five engines
each, and no comparison on ten samples separates the families statistically. This is a fleet
design limitation, not a method result: SynCFM56 v1.1 (more episodes per engine) is queued
as the F5 prerequisite for hypothesis H2.

\section{The hybrid experiment: a negative result worth having}
\label{sec:ai-hybrid}

The stacking mechanism of contribution C3 --- feeding the Kalman-GPA tracked health
parameters (10 physics-based channels) to the GRU alongside the raw deviations --- was run
through the H4 data-efficiency protocol: pure vs.\ hybrid at 10/25/100\,\% of the training
engines, three seeds each (\cref{fig:hybrid}).

\begin{figure}[htbp]
  \centering
  \includegraphics[width=0.72\textwidth]{hybrid_ablation}
  \caption{Data-efficiency ablation (mean $\pm$ std over three seeds): the stacked hybrid is
  \emph{worse} than the pure GRU everywhere, and worst exactly where H4 predicted it would
  help most. Auto-generated.}
  \label{fig:hybrid}
\end{figure}

The result is the opposite of hypothesis H4's prediction: the hybrid is worse at every data
fraction, and \emph{most} worse in the scarce-data regime (2\,918 vs.\ 1\,980 cycles RMSE at
10\,\%). The mechanism is intelligible in hindsight: the Kalman estimates are heavily smeared
(\cref{sec:trad-results}) --- they add ten noisy, mutually correlated channels whose
information content about RUL is already present in the three deviations they were computed
from. With seven training engines, ten extra input dimensions are pure variance. Two more
observations from the same figure: the pure GRU at 25\,\% of the data nearly matches 100\,\%
(1\,277 vs.\ 1\,217) --- the RUL task saturates around 17 engines on this fleet --- and seed
variance at 10\,\% is large, exactly why F5 prescribes multi-seed reporting.

This does not close hypothesis H4 --- stacking is one of three planned hybrid mechanisms, and
none has been tuned --- but it is recorded now, before the pre-registration freeze, as v0
evidence \emph{against} the popular assumption that physics features automatically help.
Negative results of this kind are precisely what the pre-registered protocol exists to keep
honest.

\section{Status toward milestone H4}

Done: the shared datamodule, detection (two variants), diagnosis, RUL with conformal
intervals, the stacking hybrid with the data-efficiency ablation, all under the common
protocol. Pending before H4 is declared: the remaining hybrid mechanisms
(physics-constrained loss, twin-residual features), SHAP attributions with the
Physics-Consistency Score (blocked on fleet v1.1 --- attribution analysis of a
sample-starved classifier would be theater), and the detection redesign. The gate requires
the AI to beat the traditional pipeline on at least one primary metric per task on
validation; prognosis already clears it, detection does not yet.
